A Day in the Life

Meet Priya, VP of Customer Operations.

She manages order fulfilment for 340 enterprise accounts across 60 countries at GlobalTech Industrial. On a Tuesday morning, she discovers that the OTC Agent already resolved a crisis she didn't know existed.

👩‍💼
Priya Nair — VP, Customer Operations
GlobalTech Industrial  ·  340 enterprise accounts  ·  60 countries  ·  $2.8B active order book
1

The Order

Salesforce CRM → SAP S/4HANA → aATP PAC

Monday, 9:14 AM. Apex Industries — GlobalTech's largest account — places a $4.2M strategic order for precision actuators through the Salesforce customer portal. S/4HANA receives the order and immediately triggers aATP's Product Availability Check (PAC), which queries stock across three warehouses, evaluates concurrent order demand, and applies Apex's Supply Protection tier. PAC returns a confirmed quantity and committed date: 18 days. A production order is created automatically. The sales team logs the win. Everything looks green.

2

The Risk

Databricks ML → SAP BTP Event Stream → OTC Agent

Wednesday, 2:31 AM. Databricks detects an anomaly: a logistics disruption at GlobalTech's primary component supplier in Penang has created a 9-day shortfall on three critical SKUs in the Apex order. This is an external signal — it exists in logistics telemetry before it has propagated into SAP inventory records. aATP's scheduled Backorder Processing run would eventually surface it, but not for hours. Databricks catches it first, pushes the event to BTP, and the OTC Agent picks up the signal immediately.

⚡ The signal reaches the OTC Agent 72 hours before it would appear in aATP's standard Backorder Processing run — because it hasn't yet touched SAP data. This is exactly the gap the agent fills.
3

The Decision

OTC Agent → aATP ABC & Supply Protection → Salesforce CRM

The agent doesn't reason about supply paths on its own — it calls aATP's Alternative-Based Confirmation (ABC) to surface every viable fulfilment alternative across the global network. aATP ABC evaluates plant substitution (Rotterdam DC: 850 units available), storage location substitution, and product substitution rules — and returns a ranked set of confirmation proposals. The agent then layers in what aATP cannot see: Apex's SLA penalty exposure ($2.1M), their relationship score in Salesforce, and the cost premium of each path. It selects the option that satisfies aATP's supply constraints and the business context:

Agent Evaluation — 4 Options  ·  Completed in 41 seconds
✓ Selected aATP ABC result: Partial shipment from Rotterdam hub (8 of 12 SKUs on day 18) + expedited air freight for critical components. Net delay to full delivery: 2 days. Cost premium: $42K. Within aATP's autonomous action policy limits. Apex SLA preserved on primary line items.
Rejected No intervention — full order delay of 9 days past committed date. aATP BOP would eventually reconfirm at the later date. Apex SLA breach: $2.1M penalty + churn risk. Rejected on business grounds.
Rejected aATP ABC result: Product substitution with alternative SKU. Substitute not on Apex's approved spec sheet in Salesforce. Engineering sign-off required (5+ business days). Timeline incompatible — rejected.
Rejected Full expedited air freight for entire order. Eliminates delay completely but cost is $380K. Exceeds aATP's Supply Protection autonomous action threshold for this order tier — CFO approval required. Rejected.
4

The Action

aATP ABC + BOP  ·  Salesforce  ·  ServiceNow

Wednesday, 2:33 AM. Two minutes after detecting the risk, the agent executes simultaneously across three systems — each action grounded in the decisions aATP already made.

In aATP, the agent commits the ABC alternative confirmation: it calls aATP.abc.commitAlternative() to update the delivery schedule with the Rotterdam partial shipment, then aATP.bop.triggerRun() to reprocess all other orders affected by the same Penang shortfall — ensuring the full order book is reconfirmed, not just Apex's. An expedited freight PO is raised within pre-approved supplier and price guardrails.

In Salesforce, it drafts and sends a proactive communication to Apex's VP of Procurement — 6 days before the original delivery date — explaining the disruption clearly, the revised delivery plan, and a $15K service credit as a goodwill gesture. The message reads like it came from Priya's team. Because, in a sense, it did.

In ServiceNow, it creates a P1 logistics coordination ticket, assigns it to the right regional operations team, and links it to the Apex account record for full audit visibility.

Priya receives a one-paragraph summary notification. No action required.

The Outcome

Result — 6 days later

Apex's VP of Procurement responds within the hour — appreciating the transparency and approving the revised plan without escalation. The primary shipment from Rotterdam lands on revised schedule. The remaining components arrive 2 days later via air freight. Apex's assembly line starts on time.

Priya reads the full resolution summary over her morning coffee. A problem that would have taken her team 3 days to detect and multiple cross-functional calls to resolve was identified, reasoned through, and executed autonomously — in under 4 minutes, in the middle of the night, with zero human intervention required.
